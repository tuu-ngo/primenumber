About Prime Numbers

A prime number is a natural number greater than 1 that is divisible only by 1 and itself.

Examples: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13 ...

This checker uses trial division up to √n, which works correctly for all integers up to 9,007,199,254,740,991.
